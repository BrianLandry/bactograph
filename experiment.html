Preparation Day

Melt Media

Take the media tube and submerge it in a container of boiling water until it completely liquefies (approximately 10 minutes). Store the melted media withe the container in a 37 degree incubator. This can be done any time at least 2 hours prior to the experimental day protocol, since the media simply has to cool to 37 degrees before use.

Set Up Lights

If the light chamber did not come constructed follow these steps. After it is constructed place it in the incubator.

  1. Line up the 4 bottom pieces in parallel. The edges labelled ‘top’ are near the top.

  2. Slide the grooves of the pegged pieces into the bottom grooves, such that the pegged pieces are perpendicular to the downside pieces. The edges labelled ‘top’ should be rightside up, so that a grid is formed. All square pegs should face up.

  3. The cover should click onto the grid, with each of the small square pegs of the grid fitting into the square holes of the top cover. The LEDs should shine into the chamber.

Culture Bacteria

Using a sterile object (pipette tips work) scrap bacteria from the hole in the agar stab and then shake off the tip in the culture media. Place the culture media on its side in a 37 degree incubator and allow it to grow for 48 hours.

Experimental Day

Prepare supplies

The class needs the following:

  • Bacterial culture
  • Melted photography media
  • Tryptophan solution

Each group needs the following:

  • Pipette
  • Culture dish
  • Ice water bath
  • Transparency section
  • Permanent marker

Add bacteria to photography media

Remove the photography media and water container from the incubator. Keeping the photography in the water container will keep it warm and prevent it from solidifying for approximately 30 minutes. Take the bacterial culture and pour it into the photography tube.

Add tryptophan

Pour the tryptophan solution into the photography media.

Mix in bacteria

Gently invert the photography media 10 times to mix the cells and tryptophan thoroughly.

Cast plate

Add approximately 3mL of photography media to each petri dish, this can be done with a pipette or by careful pouring. Quickly rotate the plate so that media is spread evenly, waiting too long will cause the media to solidify.

Add approximately 3mL of photography media to each petri dish, this can be done with a pipette or by careful pouring. In the biotechnology extension add your chosen amount of tryptophan. We suggest between 0 to 0.6mL of the 10g/L tryptophan solution per photography. Quickly rotate the plate so that media is spread evenly, waiting too long will cause the media to solidify.

Solidify media

Allow the plate to sit uncovered for approximately 5 minutes to allow the media to solidify. Gently tilt the plate to insure the media is solidified before proceeding. If the media will not solidify carefully let it rest in an ice water bath.

Attach image and incubate

Draw the picture you want to take on a piece of transparency and cut it out. Cap the the petri dish and flip it upside down. Attach the cut out piece of transparency to the petri dish using clear tape. Place the dish into an illuminated incubator.

View bacterial photograph

After incubating for at least 24 hours remove the petri dish from the incubator. Detach the transparency and admire your bacterial photograph!

Analyze results: Biotechnology Extension

Use the biotechnology worksheet to analyze the effect of tryptophan concentration on pigment production.

Analyze results: Cell Signalling

Use the cell signalling worksheet to analyze the light sensing capabilities of the bacterial photography strain.
