Preparation Day

Melt Media

Take the media tube and submerge it in boiling water until it completely liquefies (approximately 10 minutes). Store the melted media in the 37 degree incubator. This can be done any time at least 2 hours prior to the experimental day protocol, since the media simply has to cool to 37 degrees before use.

Set Up Lights

If the light chamber did not come constructed follow these steps. After it is constructed place it in the incubator.

  1. Line up the 4 bottom pieces in parallel. The edges labelled ‘top’ are near the top.

  2. Slide the grooves of the pegged pieces into the bottom grooves, such that the pegged pieces are perpendicular to the downside pieces. The edges labelled ‘top’ should be rightside up, so that a grid is formed. All square pegs should face up.

  3. The cover should click onto the grid, with each of the small square pegs of the grid fitting into the square holes of the top cover. The LEDs should shine into the chamber.

Culture Bacteria

Using a sterile object (pipette tips work) scrap bacteria from the hole in the agar stab and then shake off the tip in the culture media. Place the culture media in the 37 degree incubator and allow it to grow for 24 hours.

Experimental Day

Prepare supplies

Add bacterial media

Use the pipette to transfer two volumes of bacterial media from the class supply to your petri dish. Transfer the media by fully depressing the bulb of the pipette and sucking up the media, then fully depress the bulb over the petri dish dispensing the liquid. Do this transfer twice for two pipette volumes. Make sure to transfer to the bottom of the petri dish (smaller side)!

Add bacteria

Use the pipette to transfer one volume of bacteria from the class bacterial culture to your petria dish. Use the same method described in the previous step, however just transfer one volume.

Mix in bacteria

Mix the bacteria into the bacterial media by slowly swirling your petri dish for 15 rotations. If any bubbles have formed use the pipette to pop them or suck them up.

Solidify media

Place the lid on the petri dish. Solidify the media by placing the petri dish in 1/4 inch deep ice water and holding the dish down (and level) for 5 minutes. Insure the media has solidified by tilting the petri dish.

Attach image and incubate

Draw the picture you want to take on a piece of transparency and cut it out. Flip the petri dish upside down and attach the cut out piece of transparency to the petri dish using clear tape. Place the dish into an illuminated incubator.

View bacterial photograph

After incubating for at least 24 hours remove the petri dish from the incubator. Detach the transparency and admire your bacterial photograph!
